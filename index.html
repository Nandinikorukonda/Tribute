BIOGRAPH
Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, known as Mahatma Gandhi, was a preeminent leader of India's independence movement against British rule. Born in 1869 in Porbandar, he advocated for nonviolent civil disobedience and inspired millions with his philosophy of Satyagraha. Gandhi led various campaigns for civil rights and freedom, including the Salt March and Quit India Movement. His efforts culminated in India's independence in 1947, but his legacy extends far beyond, influencing movements for civil rights and freedom worldwide. He was assassinated in 1948, but his principles of nonviolence and peaceful resistance continue to inspire generations.

1869-1948

Dr.B.R.Ambedkar

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, an eminent freedom fighter, was the architect of India's Constitution, advocating for equality and justice. His tireless efforts empowered Dalits and oppressed communities, challenging the caste system's grip on society. Ambedkar's vision extended beyond India, advocating for human rights on the global stage. He emphasized education as a tool for emancipation, founding educational institutions to uplift marginalized sections. His seminal work on social reform and political philosophy continues to shape modern India's ethos. Ambedkar's legacy remains a beacon for those fighting against discrimination and inequality worldwide, inspiring generations to strive for a more just society.

1891-1956

Lal Bahadur shastri

Lal Bahadur Shastri, India's second Prime Minister, was a stalwart leader known for his simplicity and integrity. He coined the slogan "Jai Jawan Jai Kisan" (Hail the Soldier, Hail the Farmer) to uplift both the armed forces and the agricultural sector. Shastri's leadership during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1965 showcased his commitment to national security and peace. Despite his short tenure, his policies focused on social justice and economic development, leaving a lasting impact on the nation. His emphasis on self-sufficiency in food production laid the groundwork for India's agricultural progress. Shastri's legacy as a compassionate and principled leader endures in the hearts of millions.

1904-1966

Jawaharlal Nehru

Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister, was a towering figure in the country's independence movement and a champion of democracy. His visionary leadership shaped India's secular and socialist identity, emphasizing education, science, and technology for national progress. Nehru's commitment to social justice and non-alignment on the global stage earned him international acclaim. He laid the foundation for India's democratic institutions, promoting pluralism and inclusivity. Nehru's influence extended beyond politics, as a prolific writer and thinker, he left a rich intellectual legacy. His contributions to nation-building and his role in shaping modern India are widely celebrated and revered.

1889-1964

Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh, a revolutionary freedom fighter, remains an enduring symbol of courage and sacrifice in India's struggle for independence. His unwavering commitment to overthrowing British colonial rule inspired millions across the nation. Singh's daring acts, including the Lahore Conspiracy, aimed to ignite the spirit of rebellion against oppression. He embraced martyrdom at a young age, symbolizing his dedication to the cause of freedom. Singh's ideals of socialism and secularism continue to resonate, inspiring generations to fight against injustice. His legacy serves as a reminder of the power of youth activism and the pursuit of a just society.

1907-1931

Subhash Chandra Bose

Subhash Chandra Bose, fondly known as Netaji, was a charismatic and dynamic leader in India's struggle for independence. He fervently opposed British rule and advocated for complete freedom through his Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army). Bose's slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom" galvanized the masses to join the fight for liberation. His audacious escape from house arrest to seek support from Axis powers during World War II exemplified his determination. Despite his mysterious death, Bose's legacy as a fearless patriot and champion of India's sovereignty endures, inspiring patriotism and national pride.

1897-1945
